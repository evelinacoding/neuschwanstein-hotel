A Taste of the Mountains

Nestled in the Bavarian Alps, the cuisine around Neuschwanstein Castle is as hearty and comforting as the landscape itself. Local dishes highlight mountain flavors—fresh trout from Alpine lakes, wild venison and boar from the surrounding forests, and earthy mushrooms gathered from wooded slopes. Rustic staples like spätzle, warm potato salad, and red cabbage are elevated by rich gravies and berry sauces made from lingonberries and apples grown nearby. Sweet endings often feature airy kaiserschmarrn or apple strudel, paired with a glass of crisp Bavarian beer or fruity schnapps. It’s a cuisine that blends royal tradition with the soul of the mountains.
